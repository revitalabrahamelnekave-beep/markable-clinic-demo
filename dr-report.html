Unscheduled spotting in the first 3–6 months of continuous combined therapy is common and often settles. Persistent or unexplained unscheduled bleeding is also a recognized reason clinicians choose to evaluate sooner. General guidance (ACOG / The Menopause Society) considers evaluation for bleeding that persists beyond ~6 months, any heavy or postcoital bleeding, or bleeding after a bleed-free interval.
